I expect you to engage with these tasks / learning activities in small groups, but participation is ungraded. The more you put in, the more you get out.

The lab session will include time for discussion. Be prepared to share some of your responses to the questions in Part 1: Think and your work on problems in Part 2: Do with members of your lab group. You should also be prepared to debrief your learning process more generally, for example by discussing challenges or breakthroughs that you, or others in your lab group, are experiencing.

Debriefing is a structured process designed to reflect on the learning experience itself. In this case, debriefing sessions will focus on processing what has just happened during the lab with explicit consideration of the learning objectives or real-world implications.
